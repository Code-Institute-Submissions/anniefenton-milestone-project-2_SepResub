Hikes.

Featured here are our favourite tried-and-tested routes around the UK. Hover over each map marker to see the name, location, and difficulty of the route. Further information on the hikes can be found below.

Our difficulty ratings

Novice

Suitable for beginner or inexperienced hikers. These routes are mainly flat, with little elevation. Perfect for families.

Intermediate

These routes are a bit more challenging for the more experienced hiker. They will be strenuous but you won't need to be mountain experts to enjoy them.

Expert

Only for the experienced hiker with lots of experience hiking in different environments and conditions. You must be comfortable navigating with a map and compass.

Routes

A - Yr Wyddfa (Snowdon)
Wales - Intermediate - 6 hours

The largest mountain in Wales situated in Snowdonia. Surrounded by beautiful scenery and lovely wild swimming spots.

William in Bath - "Definitely worth the effort in getting to the top! Be careful with the changing weather - it can be quite unpredictable!"

B - Cheddar Gorge
England - Novice - 2 hours

A quirky little route featuring gorges and caves. The town of Cheddar is also worth a visit

Sandra in London - "Our kids absolutely loved this place. So interesting and lots of fun stuff to do!"

Route information
C - Pavey Ark (Jack's Rake)
England - Expert - 8 hours

For only the bravest of hikers. This route features a lot of scrambling so is only suitable for very experienced hikers.

Annie in Cardiff - "Wow this was tough and quite scary. But well worth doing if you have the ability to"

Route information
D - The Quiraing
Scotland - Intermediate - 5 hours

A must-visit on the Isle of Skye. This iconic mountain features views across the Island and can be hiked within 5 hours.

Christinah in Cheshire - "We were so excited to climb this on holiday - even our teenage children enjoyed it too!"

Route information
E - The Giant's Causeway
Ireland - Novice - 2 hours

The beautiful and natural geometric rock formations here will have you mesmerised. Suitable for all the family and an area you will never forget.

Adam in Belfast - "A perfect place to visit on a day trip. We really enjoyed it!"

Route information
F - The South Downs Way
England - Novice - 2 hours

You can do as little or as much of this route as you want. There are plenty of pub stops along the way with views over the gorgeous coastline.

Nasima in Southampton - "I love that this route is right on my doorstep. The best thing is that I never get bored of it!"

Route information
G - Carrauntoohil
Ireland - Expert - 7 hours

This is the highest mountain on the island of Ireland, and not for the faint hearted. This is a strenuous route with some scrambling areas. The view from the top is well worth the effort.

Riitta in Glasgow - "Very much a tough mountain to conquer. We were caught out by the changing weather so make sure you have all the right gear with you"

Route information
H - Pen Y Fan
Wales - Novice - 3 hours

Situated in the Brecon Beacons north of Cardiff, this is an iconic route that all those visiting Wales must do. There are multiple routes to choose to complete.

Dan in Cardiff - "Pretty much everyone I know has climbed this, and keeps climbing it! It can get quite busy in summer but regardless it's lovely to visit"

Route information
I - Ben Nevis
Scotland - Expert - 8 hours

The highest mountain in the UK, this will test you to your limits. Ben Nevis was once an active volcano millions of years ago and will take the whole day to complete.

David in Leamington Spa - "I'd expected this to be tough but I underestimated how hard it would be. You need plenty of rations to tackle this one but nevertheless it was a fantastic climb"

J - Lud's Church
England - Novice - 1 hour

An interesting little route in the heart of the Peak District. The site is a deep, rocky chasm oozing with history and atmosphere. Suitable for the whole family.

Tawhida in Norfolk - "We would never have come across this without Trek Ahead! Such an amazing place and perfect for our little ones"
